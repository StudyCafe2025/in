Interference

Interference is a phenomenon observed when two or more coherent light waves overlap and produce a resultant intensity distribution due to their phase relationships. Depending on whether the interference is constructive or destructive, bright and dark fringes are observed.

Mathematical Conditions

  • Constructive Interference: Δ = nλ, δ = 2nπ
  • Destructive Interference: Δ = (2n+1)λ/2, δ = (2n+1)π

Examples

  • Colors in soap bubbles
  • Oil films on water
  • Newton’s rings
  • Young’s double slit experiment

Thin Film Interference

Light reflected from the top and bottom surfaces of a thin film can interfere, creating colorful patterns. The interference depends on the thickness of the film, refractive index, and wavelength of light.

Newton’s Rings

A pattern of concentric rings produced by interference of light between a spherical surface and an adjacent flat surface.

Condition for dark fringe: 2t = nλ

Condition for bright fringe: 2t = (2n−1)λ/2

Diffraction

Diffraction is the bending of waves around the corners of an obstacle or aperture. The effect becomes significant when the size of the aperture is comparable to the wavelength.

Types of Diffraction

  • Fresnel Diffraction: Occurs when the source or screen is at a finite distance from the aperture.
  • Fraunhofer Diffraction: Occurs when both the source and screen are at infinity (or effectively collimated with lenses).

Single Slit Diffraction

The central maximum is the brightest and widest. The intensity falls off rapidly for subsequent fringes.

Condition for minima: a sin θ = mλ (m = ±1, ±2, ...)

Double Slit Diffraction

Combines diffraction and interference. The envelope is given by single slit diffraction and the inner fringes by interference.

Intensity formula: I = I₀ (sinα/α)² cos²β

Polarization

Polarization refers to the orientation of the oscillations of the electric field vector in a transverse wave like light.

Types of Polarization

  • Linear Polarization: Electric field oscillates in a single plane
  • Circular Polarization: Electric field rotates in a circle as the wave propagates
  • Elliptical Polarization: General case where electric field describes an ellipse

Methods to Achieve Polarization

  • Reflection
  • Refraction
  • Dichroism
  • Double refraction (birefringence)

Devices

  • Nicol Prism: Used to produce and analyze polarized light
  • Wave Plates: Half-wave and quarter-wave plates change the state of polarization

Engineering Applications

  • Measurement of refractive index
  • Measurement of wavelength of light
  • Anti-reflection coatings
  • Polarized sunglasses and 3D movie glasses
  • Optical instruments like interferometers
  • CD/DVD reading using interference
  • Strain analysis in materials
