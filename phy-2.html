Introduction

  • Matter exists in three states: gas, liquid, and solid.
  • In gases and liquids, atoms move freely without a fixed position.
  • In solids, atoms occupy fixed positions, possibly in regular patterns.
  • Solids are classified as:
    • Crystalline Solids: Regular and periodic arrangement of atoms.
    • Amorphous Solids: Irregular arrangement, like glass or plastic.

Lattice and Basis

Lattice: A geometric arrangement of points showing atom positions in space.

Space Lattice: Three-dimensional arrangement of lattice points.

Basis: A group of atoms associated with each lattice point.

Combining lattice and basis forms the crystal structure:

Crystal Structure = Lattice + Basis

Unit Cell & Lattice Parameters

A unit cell is the smallest portion of a crystal lattice that retains the overall characteristics of the crystal.

  • Lattice parameters:
    • Lengths: a, b, c
    • Angles: α (between b & c), β (between a & c), γ (between a & b)
  • These define the shape and symmetry of the unit cell.

Crystal Systems

Based on variations in lattice parameters, crystals are classified into 7 systems:

  • Cubic: a = b = c, α = β = γ = 90°
  • Tetragonal: a = b ≠ c, α = β = γ = 90°
  • Orthorhombic: a ≠ b ≠ c, α = β = γ = 90°
  • Monoclinic: a ≠ b ≠ c, α = γ = 90°, β ≠ 90°
  • Triclinic: a ≠ b ≠ c, α ≠ β ≠ γ ≠ 90°
  • Hexagonal: a = b ≠ c, α = β = 90°, γ = 120°
  • Rhombohedral: a = b = c, α = β = γ ≠ 90°

Bravais Lattices

Each crystal system can have one or more types of Bravais lattices. There are 14 distinct Bravais lattices:

  • Primitive (P): Atoms at corners only
  • Body-Centered (I): One atom at the center of the cell
  • Face-Centered (F): Atoms at each face center
  • Base-Centered (C): Atoms at center of opposite faces

These arrangements vary depending on the crystal system they belong to.

Basic Definitions: Cubic Crystal

  • Number of Atoms per Unit Cell (n):
    • Simple Cubic (SC): 1
    • Body-Centered Cubic (BCC): 2
    • Face-Centered Cubic (FCC): 4
  • Atomic Radius (r): r = a / 2 for SC
  • Coordination Number (N): Number of nearest neighbors
    • SC: 6
    • BCC: 8
    • FCC: 12
  • Atomic Packing Factor (APF): Fraction of volume occupied by atoms
    • SC: 0.52
    • BCC: 0.68
    • FCC: 0.74

APF Formula: (Number of atoms × Volume of each atom) / Volume of unit cell
